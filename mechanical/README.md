# Mechanical Design

This folder contains the mechanical and structural design information for the smart glove project.
# Structure Notes

This document describes the main mechanical parts of the smart glove project.

## 1. Four-Finger Back-of-Hand Support

This part is the main support structure for the four fingers placed on the back of the hand.

Its role is to hold the finger sensing elements in stable positions and provide the main mechanical base for the finger control area. It helps maintain the alignment of the four finger-related components and improves the overall stability of the glove during movement.

## 2. Thumb Extension Support

This part is designed for the thumb area.

Because the thumb is positioned differently from the other four fingers, a dedicated extension support is used to connect and hold the thumb-related sensing structure. This design makes the thumb integration more practical and better adapted to the real shape and motion of the hand.

## 3. Flex Sensor Ring

This part is used to support and retain the flex sensor.

Its role is to help position the flexible sensor correctly and keep it attached to the mechanical structure. It also improves the stability of the sensing element during repeated finger motion.

## 4. Palm Support and IMU Mount

This part is used to fix the palm-side structure and hold the IMU module.

Its role is to provide a stable support area for the hand and ensure that the IMU module remains properly positioned for measuring hand orientation and acceleration. This part is important for combining finger sensing with global hand motion sensing.

## General Design Purpose

Together, these four parts form the main mechanical structure of the glove. Their purpose is to support the sensors, improve structural stability, and make the full glove system easier to assemble and use.
